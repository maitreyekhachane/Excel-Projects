Project Overview:
The project aims to analyze ride booking data to understand customer behavior, service performance,
and revenue trends across different ride categories. The dataset includes information related to ride services, 
trip duration, distance traveled, fare details, payment methods, and ride status. 
The primary objective is to identify demand patterns, evaluate service-wise performance, analyze revenue generation, 
and uncover opportunities to improve operational efficiency and business profitability.

Problem Statement
The objective of this project was to analyze ride data and identify patterns in service usage, revenue generation, customer preferences, and demand trends. 
The analysis aimed to answer key business questions such as:
Which ride service generates the highest revenue?
Which services are most preferred by customers?
What are the peak revenue periods?
Which payment methods are used most frequently?
How does demand vary across services and routes?
What opportunities exist to improve profitability and service performance?

KEY INSIGHTS:
1.Bike service dominates the business with the highest ride volume (13,567) and highest revenue contribution (~30%).
2.Bike Lite has the lowest performance with only 4,388 rides and ~9.7% revenue share, indicating low customer demand. 
3.Revenue peaked in July (~50%), compared to June (~22%) and August (~27%), showing strong seasonal variation. 
4.Daily revenue trend remains stable with minor fluctuations, indicating consistent demand over time. 
5.QR Scan and GPay are the most preferred payment methods across services. 
6.Revenue and ride distribution are concentrated in a few services, indicating dependency on Bike and Auto.
